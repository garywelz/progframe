the meta-analysis finding (t=-3.34, p=0.002)?

APPROACH: Systematic literature search for genes in the K562
benchmark (780 genes) that participate in documented bistable
circuits with published evidence of positive feedback.

RESULT: EXPANSION WEAKENS THE SIGNAL
======================================================================

With original 14 Class III genes:
  Mean r (Class III): 0.7454
  Mean r (Class I):   0.7793
  C3 < C1 in 11/15 methods
  t-test: t=-3.34, p=0.002

With expanded 22 Class III genes (14 original + 8 new):
  Mean r (Class III): 0.7831  <-- essentially equal to Class I
  Mean r (Class I):   0.7793
  C3 < C1 in 9/15 methods
  t-test: t=0.41, p=0.66     <-- NOT SIGNIFICANT

The 8 new genes have HIGHER accuracy (mean r=0.849) than Class I.
The expansion destroys the statistical signal.

======================================================================
INTERPRETATION
======================================================================

The 8 new genes participate in documented bistable circuits but do NOT
show reduced prediction accuracy. Two explanations:

1. CONTEXT: FOXL2/SOX9 is a sex determination switch — not active in
   K562 leukemia cells. SRF-MRTF is a fibroblast circuit. The bistable
   behavior exists in other contexts but not here.

2. TRANSIENT vs PERSISTENT BISTABILITY: The mitotic switch genes
   (ESPL1, FBXO5, BORA, INCENP, PPP1CA) participate in cell cycle
   bistable switches that every K562 cell passes through every
   division. Models have training examples of cells in both states.
   The "hidden attractor" problem doesn't apply because both states
   are well-sampled in the data.

   By contrast, the original 14 include persistent cell-identity
   bistable circuits (GATA1/PU.1, MYC autoactivation) where the
   attractor state is a stable cell fate, not a transient cell cycle
   phase. Grammar-blind models cannot determine which fate attractor
   a cell is in from the snapshot alone.

CONCLUSION: The Class III difficulty effect is specific to PERSISTENT
bistable circuits (cell fate / identity switches), not to all positive
feedback circuits. The original N=14 set captures the relevant biology.
Expanding to include transient bistable circuits dilutes the signal.

This is itself a refinement of the hypothesis — and a finding worth
reporting in the paper.
