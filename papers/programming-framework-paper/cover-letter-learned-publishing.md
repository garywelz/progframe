Dear Editors,

I am pleased to submit for your consideration an Original Article entitled "The Programming Framework: A General Method for Process Analysis Using LLMs and Mermaid Visualisation." The paper introduces a repeatable method for turning textual process descriptions into structured, computable diagrams using large language models and Mermaid syntax, and develops the methodology through its two primary application programmes: biology (Genome Logic Modelling Project) and mathematics (Algorithms, Axiomatic Theories, and Proofs).

*Learned Publishing* is a natural home for this work. The Programming Framework speaks to a challenge at the heart of the journal's mission: making research processes easier to reproduce, scrutinise, and reuse. The outputs are version-controlled, machine-readable process artefacts—diagrams with JSON-based storage and provenance metadata in our pipeline—so they function as research infrastructure rather than static illustrations. That places the article at the intersection of scholarly communication, knowledge representation, and AI-assisted research workflow: live issues for your readers in publishing and information science. The manuscript also addresses AI transparency directly: LLM use in diagram generation is disclosed in the Acknowledgements, and the workflow is designed so that each artefact retains a traceable revision history and source attribution.

This manuscript reports original work and is not under consideration elsewhere. I am the sole author and approve submission. My ORCID is https://orcid.org/0009-0005-7806-0892, as on the manuscript.
