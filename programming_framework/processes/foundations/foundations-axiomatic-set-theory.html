Cantor & cardinality (historical thread). See Chart 7 for the dedicated flowchart. Cantor showed that the integers ℤ and the rationals ℚ are countable (same cardinality as ℕ), while ℝ is uncountable (Cantor’s diagonal argument, 1891; earlier non-denumerability of ℝ in 1874). Cantor’s theorem states |𝒫(S)| > |S| for every set S—in particular 2ℵ₀ > ℵ₀. These results motivate the Continuum Hypothesis (CH: there is no cardinal strictly between ℵ₀ and |ℝ| = 2ℵ₀) and the Generalized Continuum Hypothesis (GCH: for every infinite cardinal κ, 2κ = κ+). CH and GCH are independent of ZFC (Gödel’s constructible universe L; Cohen’s forcing).
